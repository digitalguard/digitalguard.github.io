Digital Identity of Everything

It's five years since Cisco shared their vision of how IoT would connect billions of devices. A lot of the message got lost in media. The focus was on the staggering numbers of connected devices rather than the real impact of connectivity.

Today we see traditional businesses across markets being disrupted by fully digital platform companies with new paths to dominance. The classic product companies' model of creating high barriers of entry has changed to an open model of lubrification of ecosystem participants to foster new value generation.

Customers and their digital identities are at the hart of new digital business models.  Businesses are in direct contact with customers, in near real time, to a degree that the customers and their devices are at the center of the business processes. 'Connecting everything' creates new requirements of managing identities of everything; Apps, services, customers, devices, and machines.

Identities comes in many shapes and forms. But the identity lifecycle can be thought in context of the five use cases Creation, Verification, Authentication, Authorization and Federation as outlined by One World Identity.

As the world becomes more connected, it also becomes more complex, especially when it comes to Identity & Access Management. Identity assurance, the ability to trust that people who want access to resources are who they say they are, is what induces the trust that is essential to working securely with multiple parties in an ecosystem.

Digital identities help protect customer data and improve the relevance and personalization of product experiences. Facilitating interactions and interoperability in the digital ecosystem simplifies transacting between apps , devices, things and customers.

Connecting everything enables new business models (like servitization) and truly personalized services for customers in many areas like mobility services, transportation, insurance & financial services.

Are you prepared to handle billions of relationships between customers, vehicles, mobile devices, charging stations, and IoT devices ?
